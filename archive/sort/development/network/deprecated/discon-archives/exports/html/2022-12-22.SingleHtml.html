Frequently-asked questions (FAQs)





How are stock prices determined?
Share prices are determined by the amount of activity in that division (or in yEAh Games as a whole), using a special formula. If there is more activity, the share price will rise for that divison. The number of shares issued by the company will also change the share price; if there are lots of shares, the prices will be lower; and vice versa.





What is a company's valuation?
A company's valuation is the amount of money that the company is worth; it is determined by adding the current price for all the shares that have been sold (it does not include unsold shares.)





When are share prices determined?
Share prices are determined at 4:00 PM EST — this means that the messages will be counted then, and any sent after will be counted towards the next day's stock — but prices & graphs may not be released until 5 or 6 PM.





What happens to shares that haven't been sold or were just cashed-out?
These shares are called 'nonexistent shares' and won't affect the company's valuation, but are instead held by the Yank — which possesses those shares' profits until they are sold. This protects the stock market from crashing because of fraudulent activity.





How do I invest and what does it do?
Investing in the yock market is an easy (and one of the few) way(s) to make significant yollars. If you predict that a divison is going to become popular, then you can invest and buy some of its shares from the Yank; then, if the price has gone up, you can sell the shares and make money. You can invest by using the command yeahinvest. You can only invest on what you have in your Yank account; you can take out a loan and get yoney, if you would like; you can exchange your physical yoney for online currency; or you can purchase the yocks in-person.





When do share prices count?
Share prices, when they are determined from 4 to 6 PM EST, are valid until the next close, the next day. This essentially means that a stock, for example, from Monday (after 4-6 PM), is the stock price for most of Tuesday (until 4-6 PM). Any purchasing that happens from the close at 4-6 to the close at 4-6 the next day will use the first day's price.





What is the 'STKMKT:YGSE' stock?
This is the added-up value of all the current stocks on the market; it helps to show you how well the stock market is performing as a whole. You cannot invest in this stock (at least, not yet!).
